I Challenge Diabetes Camp

Software Developer | Jul 2025 - Sep 2025

What I Did & Used

At I Challenge Diabetes Camp, I worked on software improvements for camper health-data workflows, with most of the work centered on making updates safer for staff to test and easier to trust during busy camp hours. I documented and helped design a Supabase-backed upload flow where field-collected CSV files are parsed in the app, inserted into a JSONB staging table, and then upserted into camper records through a database function that can grow when new CSV columns appear.

I also developed a staging environment so staff working closest to production could test changes without needing to shut down or disrupt the live server during peak hours. Alongside that, I worked through an automated email flow that retrieves each camper's most recent blood glucose reading and prepares parent or guardian updates. The documentation covers the Next.js/React upload path, Papa.parse CSV handling, Supabase client usage, SQL/RPC logic, PostgreSQL queries, server-side routes, and Nodemailer-based notifications.

This experience made the technical work feel very concrete. Schema changes, staging tables, environment variables, REST routes, and email delivery were not just abstract backend pieces; they had to fit into a real camp environment where staff needed things to work cleanly and families needed accurate information. It helped me get better at connecting backend data integrity with the day-to-day reality of people actually using the system.
