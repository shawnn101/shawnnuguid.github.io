I Challenge Diabetes Camp

Software Developer | Jul 2025 - Sep 2025

What I Did & Used

At I Challenge Diabetes Camp, I worked on software improvements for camper health-data workflows, focusing on cleaner CSV uploads, safer record updates, and clearer access to time-sensitive information for staff and families. I documented and helped design a Supabase-backed upload flow where field-collected CSV files are parsed in the app, inserted into a JSONB staging table, and then upserted into the camper records through a database function that can evolve when new CSV columns appear.

I also worked through a production-style automated email flow that retrieves each camper's most recent blood glucose reading and prepares parent or guardian updates. The documentation covers the Next.js/React upload path, Papa.parse CSV handling, Supabase client usage, SQL/RPC logic, PostgreSQL queries, server-side routes, and Nodemailer-based notifications. I kept the implementation in testing: first validating database query output without sending emails, then using a fixed test address before any real parent communication.

This experience made the technical work feel very concrete: schema changes, staging tables, environment variables, REST routes, and email delivery all had to support a real camp environment where accuracy and privacy mattered. It strengthened my ability to connect backend data integrity with practical staff workflows.
